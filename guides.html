The Google world

Google isn't one system. It's at least three.

The world

Google Business Profile (Maps and the local pack), organic Search (your actual website), and now AI Overviews pulling from both to answer a search before anyone clicks a link. Google itself says it sees more than 5 trillion searches a year. None of it happens automatically — a site built on Squarespace, Wix, or anywhere else doesn't rank just because it exists.

The competition

It's not just the shop down the street. Directory sites like Thumbtack, Angi, and HomeAdvisor already rank well for the exact same searches — a business's own site is competing with them too, in Google, in AI Overviews, and everywhere in between.

The science

For a Business Profile, Google publishes exactly what decides local ranking: relevance, distance, and prominence. For your actual website, Google's own quality rater guidelines reward E-E-A-T — Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness. The "Experience" part matters most here: Google explicitly rewards someone who's actually done the work over a page that just describes it — the same reasoning behind using tools like Gauge for the AI-answer-engine side of Sonshine's relaunch, not just traditional SEO.

The economics

This isn't abstract, and it isn't instant. Even Sonshine Glass — the real client behind the $93K signed contract covered elsewhere on this site — doesn't have every page indexed yet; getting a full site indexed and ranked is ongoing work, not a switch you flip once. But the pages that are indexed are the ones doing the work.

How they actually compare

Google, Angi, and Thumbtack aren't the same kind of thing, for either side of the transaction.

Google Angi Thumbtack
For the consumer Free — search, compare, and contact a business directly Free to browse and request quotes Free — submit a request, compare quotes
For the business No fee to appear or rank organically Pay to advertise and appear in search Pay per lead, whether or not it closes
Who else gets the lead No one — it's a direct call or form fill to you Typically sold to 3–5 pros at once Typically sold to multiple pros at once
What you're really buying Visibility you keep building on, indefinitely Visibility rented for as long as you keep paying A shot at a lead other pros already have too

This isn't only a Google problem

None of this works in isolation. A customer who can't reach anyone, or never hears back after asking for a quote, doesn't just go quiet — they sometimes leave a review that says exactly that. Reviews are a real, published Google ranking factor, folded into what Google calls prominence and trustworthiness. A bad experience doesn't just cost the one job in front of you — it can quietly work against the visibility that's supposed to bring the next ten.
